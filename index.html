Australia in the Age of AI

Who's using it, who's benefiting, and who's being left behind.

By Orlando Dylan Tan

The Scale

AI is no longer on the horizon.
It is here.

In just a few years, artificial intelligence has moved from research labs into everyday Australian life, reshaping how Australians work, study, and do business, but not at the same pace, and not for everyone.

Act 1  ·  Demand

The demand for AI skills
has grown fourfold

Since 2015, the share of Australian job postings requiring AI-related skills has quadrupled — a clear signal that employers across industries are reorganising work around AI capability.

Act II  ·  Geography

Not all of Australia
is connected

AI adoption and tech employment cluster heavily around New South Wales and Victoria, while regional and remote Australia lags significantly behind.

Act III  ·  Who Uses It

Adoption is uneven
across industries

Health, education and manufacturing lead AI adoption in Australia, while agriculture and retail lag significantly, raising questions about which workers and sectors will be left behind.

Act IV  ·  Where AI Businesses Cluster

Business processes dominate
Australia's AI landscape

Nearly half of all private Australian AI companies operate in business processes and IT infrastructure, while sectors like agriculture and hospitality remain on the periphery.

Act V  ·  Skill Exposure

Not all jobs face
equal exposure

Arts and media professionals now lead all occupations in AI skill demand — largely driven by hiring to train generative AI models. ICT professionals follow, while health workers and drivers remain almost untouched.
